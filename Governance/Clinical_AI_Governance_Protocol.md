# ⚖️ Clinical AI Governance & Safety Protocol (CAGP)
### *A Framework for Ethical Oversight and Algorithmic Clinical Safety*

Drawing on 20 years of experience in **Human Research Ethics Committees (HREC)**, **Scientific Review Committees (PC4)**, and **Clinical Governance**, I have developed this protocol to bridge the gap between technical AI performance and clinical safety requirements. 

This protocol ensures that Agentic AI tools are not only accurate but are ethically sound, legally compliant, and clinically safe for patient-facing environments.

---

## 🏛️ Pillar 1: Ethical Guardrails (The HREC Lens)
*Focus: Patient Autonomy, Justice, and Non-Maleficence*

* **Informed Algorithmic Consent:** Every AI interaction must include a clear, human-readable disclosure of the AI's role.
* **Bias Auditing (Diversity Mapping):** Utilizing my experience leading the **Cancer Council Diversity Plan Evaluation**, I mandate rigorous testing for algorithmic bias across culturally and linguistically diverse (CALD) populations.
* **Data Sovereignty:** Strict adherence to Australian Privacy Principles (APPs) and institutional data governance standards.

---

## 🛡️ Pillar 2: Clinical Safety & Fidelity (The Implementation Lens)
*Focus: Avoiding "Automation Bias" and Ensuring Clinical Accuracy*

* **Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) Requirement:** For any diagnostic or treatment recommendation (e.g., PPH detection or Cancer screening), a qualified clinician must act as the final 'Principal' to the AI 'Agent.'
* **Normalization Audit (NPT):** AI agents must undergo a **Coherence** check. If the AI's logic contradicts established clinical guidelines (e.g., Cochrane or PRISMA standards), the system must flag a "Methodological Conflict."
* **Adverse Event Logging:** Automated tracking of "near-misses" where AI logic deviated from clinical best practices.

---

## 📊 Pillar 3: Scientific Validation (The Q1 Publication Lens)
*Focus: Evidence-Based Performance*

* **UMBRELLA Validation:** AI outputs must be cross-referenced against high-fidelity evidence syntheses (Q1 Systematic Reviews).
* **AMSTAR 2 Compliance:** Any AI-generated evidence summary must be appraised using the same quality criteria used in my **CA: A Cancer Journal for Clinicians** research.
* **Explainability (XAI):** Models must provide "Chain-of-Thought" (CoT) transparency, allowing clinicians to audit the decision-making pathway.

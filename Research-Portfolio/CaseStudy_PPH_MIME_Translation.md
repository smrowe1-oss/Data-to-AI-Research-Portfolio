# 🏥 Case Study: Clinical-to-Technical Translation (MIME Grant)
### *Bridging Maternal Health Expertise and Medical Engineering*

This case study outlines my role as the **Clinical Lead and Subject Matter Expert (SME)** for a medical technology project funded by the **Monash Institute of Medical Engineering (MIME)**. The project focused on the early detection and management of **Postpartum Haemorrhage (PPH)**.

---

## 🛑 The Clinical Problem
Postpartum Haemorrhage remains a leading cause of maternal morbidity globally. The primary challenge is the **subjective nature of blood loss estimation** and the delay in recognizing physiological triggers. 

* **Clinical Stakes:** Minutes matter. Delayed recognition leads to a "cascade of intervention" that increases patient risk.
* **The Data Gap:** Vital signs often lag behind actual blood loss, and existing tools failed to normalize into the high-pressure environment of a delivery suite.

---

## 🛠️ My Role: The "Translator"
As the Clinical Expert, I was responsible for ensuring the engineering team built a tool that was **suitable** and **fiscally/clinically viable**.

### 1. Requirements Engineering (Clinical to Code)
I translated complex clinical diagnostic markers into a logic flow that engineers could use to build detection algorithms.
* **Clinical Input:** Visual cues of shock, uterine atony, and cumulative blood loss thresholds.
* **Technical Output:** Logic gates, alert parameters, and data input requirements for the prototype software.

### 2. Situational Analysis (SA) for Design
Applying **Situational Analysis**, I mapped the "Delivery Suite Arena." I identified that the tool could not be a standalone device that required extra hands; it had to integrate into the existing "Collective Action" of the midwife and obstetrician.

### 3. Normalization Audit (NPT)
I evaluated the prototype for **Coherence**—ensuring the alerts provided by the tech aligned with clinical "sense-making." If the alert didn't match what the midwife was seeing, the tool would be rejected.

---

## 🚀 Impact & Implementation
* **The MIME Grant:** Secured funding by demonstrating a clear pathway from "Clinical Need" to "Technical Feasibility."
* **Technical Translation:** Successfully moved the project from a theoretical clinical concept to a functional med-tech prototype.
* **Research Lineage:** This work began as independent research at the **University of Melbourne** and was scaled at the **Monash School of Nursing and Midwifery**.

---

## 🤖 The "Data to AI" Connection
This project serves as the blueprint for my current work in **Agentic AI**:
* **Agent Logic:** The logic used to define PPH triggers is the same logic used to build "Clinical Decision Support" agents today.
* **SME Oversight:** I act as the "Human-in-the-Loop," ensuring that AI developers don't build clinically unsafe models.

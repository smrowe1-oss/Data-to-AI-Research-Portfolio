# 🗺️ The SA-NPT Framework: Situational Analysis & Normalization in AI
### *Mapping Complexity and Ensuring Adoption in High-Stakes Clinical AI*

Successful AI implementation requires more than a functioning algorithm; it requires an intimate understanding of the clinical "situation." By integrating **Situational Analysis (SA)** with **Normalization Process Theory (NPT)**, I provide a dual-lens approach to AI governance.

---

## 🔍 Phase 1: Situational Analysis (The Landscape)
*Mapping the "Messy" Reality*

Before designing an AI agent, I utilize **Situational Analysis** to map the non-human and human elements of the clinical environment. This identifies the "invisible" barriers to AI.

* **Social Worlds/Arenas Maps:** I map the competing interests of stakeholders (e.g., Hospital Admin vs. Frontline Midwives vs. IT Security) to ensure the AI doesn't spark "arena conflicts."
* **Positional Maps:** I identify the various "positions" clinicians take regarding AI—from "Early Adopters" to "Safety Skeptics"—ensuring the AI's communication logic addresses all concerns.
* **Non-Human Actants:** I include EMRs, medical devices (like PPH monitors), and hospital policies as active participants in the situation.



---

## ⚙️ Phase 2: Normalization Process Theory (The Engine)
*Driving Routine Integration*

Once the situation is mapped, I use **NPT** to engineer the AI's "Normalization Pathway" through four constructs:

1. **Coherence:** Using SA insights to ensure the AI's "Reasoning Chains" make sense within the specific local culture.
2. **Cognitive Participation:** Framing the AI as a collaborative partner that solves the specific "pain points" identified in the Situational Map.
3. **Collective Action:** Aligning AI notifications with existing multi-disciplinary team flows (e.g., emergency PPH protocols).
4. **Reflexive Monitoring:** Creating feedback loops that allow clinicians to "appraise" the AI, preventing it from becoming a burdensome "forced" tool.
